# The Method — How Callecutioner Works

*The psychology behind the app: Benjamin Dennehy's sales system fused with Jut Meininger's Transactional Analysis from "How to Run Your Own Life," and how every piece of it is wired into the daily loop.*

---

## The one sentence

**You cannot control outcomes, only your thoughts, actions, and behaviours — so this app rewards behaviours and never outcomes, and gives you the psychological tools to work on the thoughts underneath the behaviours.**

That's the whole thing. Dennehy supplies the *what you do* (the behaviours of disqualification-first selling). Meininger supplies the *what's running you while you do it* (the tapes, drivers, and ego states of TA). Callecutioner is the training ground where the two meet, every single day.

---

## The two sources

**Benjamin Dennehy — disqualification-first selling.** The core law: you control your thoughts, actions, and behaviours. You do not control outcomes (whether they buy, whether they're nice, whether the market moves). Chasing outcomes makes you needy, and neediness makes you beg, crabwalk, and lose. So you stop selling *at* people and start disqualifying — you go looking for the *no*, and only the genuinely willing survive the process. XP in this app is awarded **only** for behaviours, **never** for outcomes (no points for revenue or deals). This rule is inviolable.

**Jut Meininger — "How to Run Your Own Life" (Transactional Analysis).** If Dennehy tells you the behaviour to run, Meininger explains why you *don't* run it — the old recordings that hijack you at the moment of action. His vocabulary is the app's vocabulary: **the tape, the five drivers, permissions, the Martian view, the Powerless Cycle, Natural Child vs Adapted Child.** (Note: we deliberately do **not** use "rackets" or "trading stamps" — those belong to other TA authors, not Meininger.)

## The TA psychology (Meininger) in depth

### Ego states and the three healthy places
TA says you're always operating from one of three ego states:

- **Parent** — recorded messages from authority. Splits into **Nurturing Parent** (genuine care, support) and **Critical Parent** (judgment, the flogging voice).
- **Adult** — the here-and-now, fact-based, rational you. No old tape running.
- **Child** — splits into **Natural (Free) Child** (spontaneous, curious, alive, authentic) and **Adapted Child** (the compliant/rebellious self that contorts to obey the Parent's demands).

On a call there are **three healthy places to operate from: Natural Child, Nurturing Parent, and Adult.** You get *hooked* when you drop into **Adapted Child** — and the five drivers *are* the Adapted Child's compliance strategies. So "which driver got hooked?" and "was I in a healthy place?" are the same question asked two ways.

### The tape
A **tape** is an old Parent / Adapted Child recording that plays so often you stop hearing it as a sentence and mistake it for reality. The Tape Check exists to catch it in the act. Key discipline: **dig past the feeling to the because-clause.** "I don't want to dial" is a *feeling report*. The tape is the sentence *after* it: "...because ____." That because-clause is the tape.

### The five drivers and their permissions
Drivers are the internalized "you must ___" commands. Each has a **permission** (the antidote that disarms it):

| Driver | The command it obeys | How it shows up on a dial | Permission (antidote) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hurry Up** | "Go faster, don't waste time" | racing the pitch, talking over silence | take my time |
| **Be Perfect** | "Get it exactly right, don't fail" | script paralysis, avoiding the dial entirely | make mistakes |
| **Please Me** | "Be liked, don't be a bother" | begging, crabwalking, needing them to like you | rely on myself |
| **Try Hard** | "Effort is worth; straining is virtue" | grinding forever, never letting yourself close | finish + succeed |
| **Be Strong** | "Show nothing, armour up" | robotic, zero curiosity, no feeling | feel + be open |

**Please Me is the begging + crabwalk engine** — the driver that does the most damage to a cold-caller, because it outsources your okay-ness to the other person's approval. Its antidote, *rely on myself*, takes the remote control back.

### The Martian view
Facts only, zero interpretation. What would a Martian with no human concepts — or a microphone and camera — actually record? "A sad man who can't get out of his own way" is **not** the Martian view; it's the tape wearing a lab coat. "A man made ten calls, then sat and typed" is. The gap between the story you write and what the camera sees **is the tape.** The Martian view makes that gap visible, and its power is that it is *flat*: not flattering, not damning. Flat facts starve the tape, which needs the adjectives to live.

### The Powerless Cycle
The collapse underneath the drivers. When the drivers run out of gas you fall into futility: **"nothing I do matters."** This is not a driver — it's the giving-up spiral, and for anyone carrying depression it's the illness's signature line, presenting itself as clarity rather than as a symptom. It deflects evidence (pile up proof and it just says "yeah, but none of it matters"). You don't break it with proof. You break it by **reclaiming power and redeciding** — permitting yourself to feel powerful and choosing that the doing matters because you say so, not because a result granted permission.

### The Island score — worth is fixed
Displayed in the app as a permanent **9**, not editable. The principle: **roles can fail; the person cannot.** "Cold caller" is a role, and roles fumble all day — that's just data. Your worth as a person is not on trial and cannot be scored. Every worth-tape ("I'm a failure," "I'm not worthy," "nothing I do matters") is an attempt to smuggle a role's bad day onto the person's permanent record. The 9 refuses the transaction. Worth is the ground you already stand on, never the prize you win by succeeding.

### Permissions and redeciding
The counter-move to a driver is a **permission** you grant yourself. Meininger's frame is that you can *redecide* — step out of the old Parent's rules and give yourself the allower (make mistakes, take my time, rely on myself, finish + succeed, feel + be open). The Tape Check ends on this deliberately: name the tape, find the driver, expose its justifications, take the Martian view, then **grant the permission.**

## The Dennehy sales spine

- **Disqualification-first.** Go looking for the no. You're not convincing anyone; you're finding out if they're a fit. This kills neediness at the root.
- **Behaviours, not outcomes.** You own the pickup, the pattern interrupt, the push-away. You never owned the yes. So that's all you score.
- **The Gauntlet (the 11-step cold-call spine):** Pattern interrupt → 30-second pitch (three problems, framed as *"they're [feeling] because [problem we solve]"*) → Push-away #1 → Is it important? → Push-away #2 → Emotional grinder → Crabwalk check → Conversational questioning → Vampire invite → Last chance to bail → Ask for a referral. (Steps 1–10 are verified from Corbin's notebook; step 11 is his addition.)
- **The three sins:** crabwalking (going sideways to dodge the hard question), begging, and being "I"-focused. All three are neediness in different clothes.
- **The bosses** (why FX deals die): Loyalty Wraith, Priority Phantom, Price Ogre, Document Dodger, The Ghost. You *grind* the boss (push into it, make them defend it) or you *fold* to it. The scorable act is whether **you** grinded or folded — your behaviour, not their reaction.
- **Ben's voice.** Blunt, assertive, unbothered, zero apology, genuinely willing to be hung up on. Never needy. A response that begs or hedges is off-voice; a response that states the truth plainly and hands the other person a real choice is on-voice. Gatekeeper and objection responses should carry that edge.

## How it integrates with the app — feature by feature

**Dawn (the 5-minute pre-dial ritual).**
- *Three gratitudes* — get into Natural Child / open state before the phone.
- *The Tape Check* — the full Meininger sequence: the tape (because-clause) → the driver pushing it (with its tell shown on tap) → the justifications it hides in → the Martian view → the permission granted. It **never gates dialing** ("Tape unclear? Write 'unclear', grant a permission, dial").
- *Natural Child Ignition* — tap the seven productive states (Relaxed, Comfortable, Alert, Eager, Energized, Excited, Powerful); the goal is all seven at once.
- *Three quests* — behaviours only, 100% yours, written as honest yes/no commitments.
- *First dial at:* — "When is the only relevant question," the direct antidote to the "I'm not ready" tape. Readiness is never required; only a start time.

**Hunt (live, during the block).**
- *Tallies* — dials, DM convos, pitches, grinders, pins pulled, meetings, NOs (with a 10-in-a-row bonus). Pure behaviour counting = Dennehy's law made physical.
- *Playbook* (Script / Objections / Questions) — his editable cold-call script (mirrors the 11 gauntlet steps), his objection wall (pushback → comeback, answer with a question), and his questions (client-questions they ask him, plus his own discovery questions to open them up). All carried in the pocket, edited on the phone.

**Card (the 90-second post-call autopsy).**
- *The Gauntlet steps* — grade each move (— not reached, ✗ missed, ~ wobbly, ✓ nailed).
- *The three sins* — did you crabwalk, beg, get I-focused.
- *Boss fight* — which boss showed up, and did **you** grind it or fold.
- *"Where did I run the call from?"* — the driver that hooked you (Adapted Child) **or** the healthy place you held (Natural Child / Nurturing Parent / Adult). This is the TA ego-state check applied to a live call.
- *Deep autopsy* — the bait (what pulled the tape), what Ben would have said, the Martian fact.
- *The One Thing* — one behaviour for the very next dial. The card cannot be filed without it. This gate is the entire point of the card.

**Dusk (the 5-minute close).**
- *Did I do what I said?* — check off the quests. Behaviour accountability, not performance.
- *Behaviour rank* — scored on what you said you'd do, nothing else.
- *Powerless Cycle Patrol* — "did the cycle get you today?" and, if yes, what were you actually hungry for and how do you take your power back (reclaim + redecide). Meininger's Powerless Cycle, operationalized.
- *How do I feel + the Island 9* — the fixed 9 sits right there so the day's feeling can't rewrite your worth.
- *Close the day / low-day close* — a bad day can be closed in one tap and still keep the streak. "A closed bad day beats an unclosed good one. The streak measures showing up to the mirror, not performance."

**Ledger (analytics).**
- *Driver Frequency* — "the tape that shows up most is your training camp." Turns your recurring Adapted-Child hook into a visible target.
- *Mastery map* — nailed-rate per gauntlet step over time.
- *Correlation chart* — behaviours done vs. how you felt, made visible. Ben's experiment, charted.

**XP & ranks** — every point is a behaviour (dial, DM, pitch, grinder, pin, no-beg meeting, NO, card filed, boss grinded, Dawn complete, quest done, powerless cycle broken). Ranks climb from *Squire of the Spread* to *THE FOREXECUTIONER*. There is no XP for a single dollar earned. On purpose.

## The tapes in practice

The Tape Check isn't theory; it's the daily excavation. In real use the tapes surface one at a time, each looking like a different problem:

- *"I don't want to dial because I'll be rejected."* → **Please Me** → rely on myself. (A no isn't rejection of you; it's disqualification of the fit.)
- *"...because it reminds me I'm a failure."* → **Be Perfect**, collapsing the role's stumble into the person's identity. → make mistakes. The role can fail; you can't.
- *"...because I don't put in effort at all."* → Be Perfect's other face: paralysis. If it can't be perfect, don't do it — so you can't fail at what you never attempted. → make mistakes unlocks doing it at all.
- *"...because I'm not worthy of success."* → **Try Hard** → finish + succeed. Worth isn't the prize you win by succeeding.
- *"...because nothing I do matters."* → the **Powerless Cycle**, not a driver → reclaim power and redecide.

The pattern underneath all of them is one tape: **"my worth is on trial here."** Different drivers are just different doors into the same room. Which means the antidote is also one thing said many ways: **your worth — the fixed 9 — is not up for grabs on this call. It never was.** Everything in the app, from the behaviour-only XP to the low-day close to the Island score, exists to make that true in practice, not just in theory.

---

## The daily loop is the practice

**Dawn** (find and disarm the tape, set behaviour quests) → **Hunt** (run the behaviours, tally them) → **Card** (autopsy the behaviours, catch the sins and the hooked driver, set the one next thing) → **Dusk** (close honestly, patrol the Powerless Cycle, keep the streak) → **Ledger** (watch the tape you fight most and the behaviours you're mastering).

You show up to the mirror, you do what's yours to do, you let the outcomes be outcomes. That's how you run your own life, and it's how you run the phones.
